09 Research & Publications

Peer-reviewed work in applied AI.

MSc Thesis · Liverpool John Moores University IEEE (submitted) June 2026

Early Stress Prediction from Wearable Physiological Signals: A Subject-Independent Comparative Deep-Learning Benchmark on the WESAD Dataset

Reframes stress detection as forecasting rather than after-the-fact classification: given multimodal physiological signals up to time t, can a model predict the stress state at t + k for k of 30, 60 and 120 seconds? A fully reproducible pipeline over 15 WESAD subjects compares a Transformer, a 1-D CNN and an LSTM against a logistic-regression baseline under strict Leave-One-Subject-Out cross-validation, with paired significance testing and a modality ablation. The benchmark itself, a subject-independent, multi-horizon reference point, is the contribution.

LSTM best mean F1 0.5518 vs CNN 0.4291 · Transformer 0.2949 LOSO cross-validation Horizons 30 / 60 / 120s Skin temperature most predictive p = 0.004, d-z = 0.90
